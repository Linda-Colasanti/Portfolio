A Note From Linda
Why this background is unusual
✦

I have spent 15+ years as a Dyslexia Specialist and Orton-Gillingham practitioner working with students that traditional classroom instruction could not reach. I am not a classroom teacher. I am the person educators call when their students are still struggling. That perspective is exactly why my AI work is different.

I am also a certified AI consultant and implementation specialist. I build AI-powered learning tools and use AI every single day in my practice. And I am telling you, from the inside, that AI alone will never close the learning gap. Because the gap is never just about skills. Underneath every skill gap is a belief gap.

This is not a theoretical background. It is a practitioner's track record built from real-world work with nontechnical learners at every level, from struggling second graders to C-suite executives who cannot figure out why their AI rollout is failing.

I hold certifications across three pillars: dyslexia and structured literacy, master-level NLP and behavioral change, and AI consulting and implementation. I was a co-architect of the coalition and legislative strategy behind North Carolina House Bill 149, signed July 20, 2017. It was the first dyslexia identification and intervention law in North Carolina history, and it passed unanimously in both chambers.
